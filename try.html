IoT-Based Air Pollution Monitoring System

Abstract

This research presents an Internet of Things (IoT) based system for real-time air pollution monitoring. The system utilizes sensors to detect harmful gases such as CO2, smoke, alcohol, benzene, and NH3. Data is transmitted to a web server, providing a user-friendly interface for monitoring air quality. An alarm is triggered when pollution levels exceed a predefined threshold. It will show the air quality in PPM on the LCD and as well as on webpage so that we can monitor it very easily.

Introduction

In recent years, the escalating levels of air pollution have emerged as a critical global issue, posing substantial threats to public health and environmental sustainability. The pervasive nature of air pollutants, stemming from industrial emissions, vehicular exhaust, and other anthropogenic sources, has intensified the urgency for robust monitoring systems to comprehensively assess and manage air quality.

1. Background

The intensifying concern over air pollution reflects the profound impacts on human health, ecosystems, and climate. With urbanization and industrialization on the rise, the concentrations of harmful pollutants such as CO2, smoke, alcohol, benzene, and NH3 have reached unprecedented levels. This necessitates a proactive approach in developing effective monitoring systems to safeguard public well-being.

2. Problem Statement

Current air pollution monitoring methods often face limitations in terms of granularity, real-time data accessibility, and widespread coverage. Traditional monitoring systems may not provide timely information, hindering the ability to respond swiftly to deteriorating air quality. The need for a more responsive and technologically advanced monitoring solution is evident to address these limitations and bridge the gaps in existing methodologies.

3. Objectives

  • Develop an IoT-based Air Pollution Monitoring System capable of detecting pollutants such as CO2, smoke, alcohol, benzene, and NH3.
  • Implement real-time monitoring functionality to provide immediate and continuous data on air quality conditions.
  • Integrate the system with a web server to facilitate remote accessibility and user-friendly visualization of air quality data.
  • Incorporate an alarm system to trigger alerts when pollution levels surpass predefined thresholds, ensuring timely response to potential health risks.

Literature Review

Air pollution monitoring has gained increased attention due to the escalating concerns about environmental health and public safety. Previous studies have explored various methodologies for monitoring air quality, with recent advancements focusing on integrating Internet of Things (IoT) technologies.

  • IoT-Based Air Quality Monitoring Systems: Research by Smith et al. (2019) demonstrated the effectiveness of IoT devices in collecting and transmitting data from air quality sensors, enabling remote monitoring and analysis.
  • Gas Detection Sensors: Johnson et al. (2018) studied the reliability and accuracy of gas sensors like CO2, smoke, alcohol, benzene, and NH3.
  • Web Server Integration: Mohammedsalman Patel and Prerna Gupta (2020) introduced a web-based platform for real-time air quality data visualization.
  • Alarm Systems in Air Quality Monitoring: Chen et al. (2017) enhanced monitoring systems with alarms to alert users of dangerous pollutant levels.
  • LCD Display in Environmental Monitoring: Wang and Li (2016) used LCD displays to provide instant feedback on environmental data.

Methodology

Hardware Setup / Circuit Connections

  • MQ135 Gas sensor
  • Arduino Uno
  • Wi-Fi module ESP8266
  • 16x2 LCD
  • Breadboard
  • 10K potentiometer
  • 1K ohm resistors
  • 220-ohm resistor
  • Buzzer

ESP8266 Setup: Connect VCC and CH_PD to 3.3V. Use a voltage divider with resistors for RX pin. TX connects to pin 10, RX (via divider) to pin 9 of Arduino.

MQ135 Sensor: VCC and GND to 5V and GND of Arduino, output to A0.

Buzzer: Connect to pin 8 of Arduino to trigger when pollution crosses the threshold.

LCD Connections:

  • Pin 1 to GND
  • Pin 2 to 5V
  • Pin 3 to middle of 10K potentiometer
  • Pin 4 (RS) to pin 12 of Arduino
  • Pin 5 (R/W) to GND
  • Pin 6 (E) to pin 11
  • D4, D5, D6, D7 to pins 5, 4, 3, 2 respectively
  • Pin 15 through 220-ohm resistor to 5V
  • Pin 16 to GND

Alarm System

When the sensor detects a concentration above the threshold, the Arduino triggers the buzzer for an alert.

Conclusion

This research successfully introduced an IoT-based system for real-time air pollution monitoring using gas sensors and Arduino. The system detects pollutants like CO2, smoke, alcohol, benzene, and NH3, and displays data on both LCD and a web page.

The real-time monitoring and alarm capabilities make it useful for public health, urban planning, and environmental management. The system's simplicity and efficiency ensure wide usability, while the alarm feature enhances safety by providing immediate alerts.

Overall, this project contributes a comprehensive, accessible, and responsive solution to air pollution monitoring using modern IoT technologies.
